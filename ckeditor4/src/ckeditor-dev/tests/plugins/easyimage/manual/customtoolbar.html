Apollo 11 was the spaceflight that landed the first humans on the Moon on July 20, 1969, at 20:18 UTC.

Armstrong spent about three and a half two and a half hours outside the spacecraft, Aldrin slightly less; and together they collected 47.5 pounds (21.5 kg) of lunar material for return to Earth.
